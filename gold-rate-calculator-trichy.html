What happened was three things that are not written on the rate board and not explained unless you ask. And most people do not ask because they do not know they should.

  • Making charges — what the jeweller charges for turning raw gold into the actual piece of jewellery. Usually a percentage of the gold value. Varies from shop to shop and sometimes apparently from customer to customer depending on how confident you look walking in.
  • Wastage or Value Addition (VA) — some jewellers add a separate wastage percentage claiming gold is lost during manufacturing. Not every shop charges this. Some include it quietly inside the making charges. The more you know, the better you can ask.
  • GST at 3% — mandatory, goes to the government, no jeweller anywhere in India can waive it. Many buyers forget to include it when estimating their budget. I forgot it three times before I stopped forgetting it.

22K vs 24K vs 18K Gold in Trichy — What I Learned After Asking Too Many Questions

For a long time I assumed 24K was always better and everyone should always buy 24K. I said this out loud at a jewellery shop in Trichy once. The jeweller smiled very patiently. My wife asked me to go wait outside.

Here is what is actually true.

24K gold — ₹— per gram in Trichy today
99.9% pure gold. Beautiful. Also extremely soft — soft enough to scratch with a fingernail. This is why nobody makes wearable jewellery in 24K. It would lose its shape within weeks. 24K is for gold coins, gold bars, and investment purposes.

22K gold — ₹— per gram in Trichy today
91.6% pure gold mixed with a small amount of copper and silver to make it strong enough to actually wear. Standard for jewellery across India. You will also see this called 916 gold — 916 out of 1000 parts are pure gold. Same thing, different name, equally confusing the first time you hear it.

18K gold — ₹— per gram in Trichy today
75% pure gold. Used mainly for diamond jewellery and studded pieces because the harder alloy holds the settings better. Lower gold content means lower price but also lower gold resale value specifically.

Always check the BIS hallmark. For 22K, look for 916 stamped on the piece. For 18K, look for 750. No hallmark is a red flag. I learned this late. You are learning it now. You are already ahead of where I was.

Making Charges in Trichy — The Number That Changed How I Shop

Making charges are the single most negotiable and least talked about part of your gold jewellery bill in Trichy. I spent years not knowing this. I am still a little annoyed about it.

The range I have seen in Trichy is genuinely wide — anywhere from 6% on simple machine-made pieces to 35% on elaborate handcrafted bridal sets. No government rule fixes this number. Every shop decides their own. Two shops on the same street can have completely different making charges for the same type of chain.

Here is what nobody told me: making charges are negotiable. Especially for plain or machine-made jewellery. I have walked into shops in Trichy, politely asked if they can do better on making charges, and had the percentage come down 2 to 3 points just like that. The jeweller knows this is negotiable. The customer usually does not. Now you do.

Also important — making charges do not come back when you sell the gold later. When you sell old gold in Trichy, the shop pays you for weight and purity only. The making charges you paid are gone. They were the price of the design and the experience. Fine. But know this going in so you are not surprised going out.

GST on Gold in Trichy — The 3% I Kept Forgetting to Budget For

I forgot to include GST in my budget on my first gold purchase in Trichy. And my second. And if I am being honest, my third. Each time the bill was slightly higher than I expected and I would do that thing where you look at the amount, look at the jeweller, look at the amount again, and then quietly pay.

GST on gold jewellery in India is 3%. This is applied on the combined total of gold value plus making charges. Not just on the gold — on everything including the labour.

On a ₹1,00,000 jewellery purchase in Trichy, GST adds ₹3,000. On a big bridal set worth ₹5,00,000, that is ₹15,000 in GST alone. Non-negotiable, goes to the government, every shop charges it. You just need to remember to budget for it — which is exactly what this calculator does automatically.

Why is the gold rate in Trichy slightly different from other cities?

Local jewellers associations in each city set their own daily rates based on the national IBJA benchmark plus local factors like transportation costs and demand. Trichy's rate is set by the Trichy Jewellers Association. The difference between cities is usually ₹10 to ₹100 per gram but it exists and it is worth knowing.

What is 916 gold and why does everyone in Trichy call it that?

916 gold is just 22K gold. The 916 means 91.6% pure gold. It is the standard purity for jewellery across India. Jewellers say 916 because it sounds precise and technical. It means the exact same thing as 22K. Once you know this, a lot of jewellery shop conversations suddenly make much more sense.

How much making charges is normal in Trichy?

For most plain gold jewellery in Trichy, 8% to 14% is a reasonable range. Above 20% should come with a clear reason — usually highly intricate handcrafted work. If a shop is quoting 25% for a plain chain with no explanation, you are allowed to ask why. You are also allowed to go to a different shop.

Is gold cheaper on Akshaya Tritiya or Dhanteras in Trichy?

The gold rate itself does not become cheaper on these days. High demand can actually push it slightly higher. What some shops offer during festive seasons is a discount on making charges — not on the gold rate. So when you see festive offers in Trichy, check specifically what the discount applies to. Making charge discounts are real and useful. Gold rate discounts on festive days are mostly marketing.

My jeweller in Trichy quoted a rate very different from what is shown here. What do I do?

First check you are comparing the same purity — 22K to 22K. Second, ask them to show you their daily rate certificate. Third, show them this page politely and ask them to explain the difference. Most good jewellers in Trichy are transparent once they know the customer is informed. If they become defensive or evasive, that tells you something useful.
